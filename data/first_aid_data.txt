Scenario: Heavy Bleeding
Description: A person is bleeding heavily due to a cut or wound.
Steps:
1. Ensure your own safety first (use gloves or a barrier if available).
2. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
3. Apply firm, direct, continuous pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or sterile dressing.
4. If blood soaks through, do not remove the cloth; add another layer or pad and maintain pressure.
5. If the person appears pale, cold, or weak (signs of shock), lay them flat and raise their feet about 12 inches (30 cm) while continuing to control the bleeding.

Scenario: Severe Burns
Description: A person has a deep or large-area burn injury.
Steps:
1. Ensure your own safety and remove the person from the heat source.
2. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
3. Cool the burn immediately under cool or lukewarm running water for at least 20 minutes. Do not use ice or butter.
4. Gently remove any clothing or jewelry from the burn area, unless it is stuck to the skin.
5. Loosely cover the cooled burn with a non-stick, sterile dressing or clean, non-fluffy cloth to prevent infection.

Scenario: Choking (Adult)
Description: A person cannot breathe, speak, or cough forcefully due to a blocked airway.
Steps:
1. Ask the person, "Are you choking?" and encourage them to cough forcefully.
2. If coughing fails, perform up to 5 sharp back blows between the shoulder blades.
3. If still choking, perform up to 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
4. Alternate between 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until the object is dislodged or the person becomes unconscious.
5. If the person becomes unconscious, gently lower them to the ground and begin CPR.

Scenario: Fainting
Description: A person suddenly loses consciousness briefly due to a temporary drop in blood flow to the brain.
Steps:
1. Gently lower the person to the ground and lay them flat on their back.
2. Elevate their legs about 12 inches (30 cm) to improve blood flow to the brain (unless a head injury is suspected).
3. Loosen any tight clothing around their neck, chest, or waist, and ensure they have plenty of fresh air.
4. If they do not recover quickly (within a minute) or if they hit their head during the fall, call emergency medical services.
5. Once conscious, let them rest before slowly helping them to sit up; offer water but only if they are fully alert.

Scenario: Dog Bite
Description: A person is bitten by a dog and the skin is broken.
Steps:
1. Ensure your safety and separate the person from the dog.
2. If bleeding is minor, thoroughly wash the wound with mild soap and warm, running water for 5 to 10 minutes.
3. Control any significant bleeding by applying gentle, direct pressure with a clean cloth.
4. Apply an antibiotic ointment and cover the wound with a clean, sterile bandage.
5. Seek immediate medical attention for any bite that breaks the skin to assess for stitches, tetanus, and rabies risk.

Scenario: Electric Shock
Description: Person received an electric shock.
Steps:
1. Do not touch the person directly. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
2. If possible and safe, switch off the power source (e.g., circuit breaker or main fuse).
3. If the power cannot be turned off, use a long, dry, non-conductive object (wood, plastic) to push the person away from the source.
4. Once separated, check for breathing and pulse. Begin CPR immediately if the person is unresponsive and not breathing.
5. Treat any burn injuries with cool, running water and keep the person still until medical help arrives.

Scenario: Snake Bite
Description: Person bitten by a snake (treat all as venomous unless certain otherwise).
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
2. Keep the person calm and still; have them lie down with the bitten limb immobilized to minimize venom spread.
3. Remove any jewelry or tight clothing from the bitten limb.
4. Immobilize the limb (e.g., with a splint or firm bandage—do not use a tourniquet or cut blood flow).
5. Do not wash the wound, cut the wound, attempt to suck out the venom, or apply ice. Transport to the hospital immediately.

Scenario: Heart Attack
Description: Person feels severe chest pain that may spread to the arms, neck, or jaw, often accompanied by shortness of breath or nausea.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
2. Help the person sit down in a comfortable position (sitting upright and leaning back is often best).
3. Loosen tight clothing.
4. If the person is conscious, not allergic to aspirin, and does not have a condition that prevents its use, give them one adult aspirin (300 mg) to chew slowly.
5. Monitor breathing and responsiveness. Be prepared to perform CPR if they become unconscious and stop breathing.

Scenario: Fracture/Broken Bone
Description: A bone is visibly deformed or the person cannot move the limb due to severe pain or trauma.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services (911/999/000).
2. Immobilize the injured area by supporting it above and below the fracture site.
3. Do not attempt to realign the bone or push protruding bones back in.
4. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth to reduce swelling.
5. Treat for shock by keeping the person warm and calm until help arrives.

Scenario: Sprain/Strain
Description: Pain, swelling, and bruising, usually in the ankle, wrist, or knee after twisting or overextending.
Steps:
1. Remember R.I.C.E. (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation).
2. Rest the injured limb and avoid putting weight on it.
3. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth for 15-20 minutes every 2-3 hours.
4. Compress the area using a soft bandage, but not so tightly that it causes numbness.
5. Elevate the limb above the level of the heart to reduce swelling.

---
Scenario: Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)
Description: Rapid onset of symptoms like difficulty breathing, swelling of the face/throat, rash/hives, or dizziness after exposure to an allergen.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000).
2. If available, help the person use their epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) by pressing it firmly against the outer thigh.
3. Help the person sit up in a comfortable position, ideally with their legs elevated (if not worsening breathing).
4. Loosen any tight clothing.
5. Monitor breathing and be prepared to start CPR if they stop breathing.

---
Scenario: Stroke
Description: Sudden numbness or weakness of the face, arm, or leg (especially on one side of the body), confusion, trouble speaking, or sudden severe headache.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000); time is critical.
2. Use the F.A.S.T. test: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency.
3. Note the exact time symptoms began.
4. Lay the person on their back with head slightly raised.
5. Do not give them anything to eat or drink.

Scenario: Heat Exhaustion/Heat Stroke
Description: Exhaustion: Heavy sweating, paleness, muscle cramps, dizziness. Stroke: Body temperature >104°F (40°C), confusion, no sweating.
Steps:
1. Move the person to a cooler place immediately.
2. Loosen clothing and lay the person flat; elevate their feet slightly.
3. Give sips of cool water or a sports drink if conscious and not nauseous (for exhaustion only).
4. For Heat Stroke (if confused or unconscious), call emergency services immediately and apply ice packs to the neck, armpits, and groin.

---
Scenario: Hypothermia
Description: Core body temperature drop (e.g., from cold exposure), symptoms include shivering, slurred speech, confusion, and loss of coordination.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services (911/999/000).
2. Move the person out of the cold and remove any wet clothing.
3. Warm the person slowly by wrapping them in dry blankets (including their head) and placing dry, warm compresses only on the chest, neck, and groin.
4. If conscious, give them warm (non-alcoholic, non-caffeinated) drinks.
5. Do not rub or massage the skin.

Scenario: Chemical Ingestion (Poisoning)
Description: A person has swallowed a toxic substance (e.g., household cleaner, bleach, strong acid/alkali) and is showing symptoms like burning throat, vomiting, or difficulty breathing.
Steps:
1. Call emergency medical services immediately (911/999/000) or a local Poison Control Center.
2. Ensure the person is breathing. If the person is unconscious or not breathing, begin CPR.
3. Keep the person calm and seated or lying on their side (recovery position) if drowsy or vomiting.
4. If the substance is known, try to save the container to show medical personnel, as it identifies the chemical ingested.
5. Do NOT try to make the person vomit, and do NOT give them water, milk, or food unless specifically instructed to do so by emergency operators or Poison Control.
